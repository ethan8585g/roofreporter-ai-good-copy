P1 — HIGH

Add LocalBusiness Schema with areaServed to All US City Pages

Your city pages have basic geo meta tags but are missing LocalBusiness schema with areaServed arrays listing nearby cities and counties. This is what Google uses for "near me" and local pack ranking. Example:

{"@type": "LocalBusiness", "areaServed": [{"@type": "City", "name": "Houston"}, {"@type": "City", "name": "Sugar Land"}]}

📈 Helps pages appear in Google's Local Pack (the map results that get ~44% of clicks).

05 Technical SEO Refinements

P2 — MEDIUM

Add VideoObject Schema to Pages with Embedded Content

If you have demo videos or tutorial content, wrap them in VideoObject schema. Google surfaces video rich results for "how to measure a roof" and similar queries — these get 41% higher click-through rates than standard blue links.

P2 — MEDIUM

Implement Dynamic Lastmod in Sitemaps

Ensure your sitemaps use actual last-modified dates from the database (not static dates). Google uses lastmod to prioritize re-crawling. Your blog sitemap should pull updated_at from the DB; cost pages should update when price data changes.

P3 — NICE TO HAVE

Add Breadcrumb Navigation to US Vertical Pages

Your cost pages have breadcrumbs, but verify all US vertical pages (insurance-claims, storm-damage, hail-damage) render visible breadcrumb navigation matching the BreadcrumbList schema. Google shows breadcrumbs in search results — they improve CTR by 10-15%.

06 Content Strategy for US Market

Key insight: The average roofing lead via Google Ads costs $186 in 2026. Organic leads cost $25–$100 after pages are established. Every rankable page you build is a compounding asset that pays for itself within months.

Content Pillars to Build

Storm Season Content (Seasonal, High Volume): "2026 Hail Season Forecast for [State]" — publish before peak season (March–July for hail belt). These rank fast because they target time-sensitive queries.

Insurance Claims Guides (Evergreen, High Intent): "How to File a Roof Insurance Claim in [State] — Step-by-Step" — your HowTo schema is perfect for this. Create for all 50 states.

Cost Comparison Content (High Volume, High Intent): "Roof Replacement Cost in [City] vs [Nearby City]" — comparative content ranks for both city names simultaneously.

Contractor Workflow Content (Mid-Funnel, SaaS Conversion): "How [City] Roofers Use Satellite Measurement to Win More Jobs" — targets contractors searching for efficiency tools.

07 US-Specific SEO Power Moves

P1 — HIGH

Submit to US Roofing Directories for Backlinks

Get listed on: Angi (Angie's List), HomeAdvisor, BBB, RoofingContractor.com, Thumbtack, BuildZoom, Porch.com. Each listing is a high-authority backlink + referral traffic source. These directories dominate roofing SERPs — being listed makes Google see you as a legitimate US player.

P1 — HIGH

Create a US Google Business Profile (if not done)

Even as a SaaS, you can create a GBP as a "service area business" covering the entire US. This gets you into Google Maps results for "roofing software" and "roof measurement tool" queries. List your service categories as "Roofing Service" and "Software Company."

P2 — MEDIUM

Target US-Specific Long-Tail Keywords

Your .ca domain inherently signals Canadian content to Google. Counter this by:

1. Using hreflang="en-US" prominently on all US pages (already done — good)
2. Including "United States" or state names in title tags
3. Using USD pricing on all US-facing pages
4. Getting backlinks from .com and .us domains
5. Consider a roofmanager.com or roofmanager.us domain that 301s to www.roofmanager.ca/us — this alone could significantly boost US organic rankings

P1 — HIGH

Consider Acquiring a .com Domain

This is perhaps the highest-leverage single action for US traffic. A .ca domain carries a geographic signal that deprioritizes it for US searchers. Even if you keep the main site on .ca, having roofmanager.com redirect (or serve US content) would remove a significant ranking handicap for American queries.

📈 .com domains rank 10-15% better than ccTLDs for non-local-country searches in Google US results.
