# RoofManager.ca — Cold Call Script (Casual)

**For: Ethan (Founder)**
**Target: Roofing contractors and solar sales companies**

---

## THE MINDSET

You're a roofer calling another roofer. You're not pitching, you're telling them about something you built because you got tired of the same stuff they're dealing with. Keep it low-key. If they're not into it, cool — move on. The confidence comes from not needing the deal.

---

## PRE-CALL PREP (2 minutes)

Before dialing, know:

1. Owner's first name
2. Where they work (their city or service area)
3. One thing that's real — a job photo, a review, something on their site
4. One weak spot you can point at if it comes up — no reviews, old site, no system, etc.

---

## THE SCRIPT

### OPENER (first 10 seconds)

> **"Hey [First Name], Ethan here — full disclosure, this is a cold call. You want to hang up, or give me 30 seconds?"**

*[Pause. Smile while you say it. The honesty disarms them — most people laugh or say "alright, go."]*

**If they laugh / say "go ahead":**

> **"Appreciate it. I'll be quick."**

**If they say "Who are you with?":**

> **"Ethan with Roof Manager — software for roofing companies. 30 seconds and you can tell me to get lost."**

**If they say "Not interested":**

> **"Fair enough — didn't even tell you what it was yet, so I'll take that as a compliment. Take care."**

*(Hang up clean. Don't push. You earned credibility by being honest — it'll pay off if you ever hit them again.)*

---

### THE HOOK (next 15 seconds)

> **"So I run Roof Manager — we work with roofing companies around [their area]. Not trying to sell you anything today, I just wanted to put it on your radar because I think it'd actually save you some time. Mind if I give you the quick version?"**

---

### THE VALUE DROP (30 seconds, conversational)

> **"Basically — you type in an address, and a few minutes later you've got a full measurement report. Square footage, pitch, ridges, hips, valleys, the whole thing. No drones, no ladder, no waiting on EagleView."**

*[Pause.]*

> **"And it's not just measurements. The rest of it's a CRM built for roofing — leads, pipeline, invoicing, follow-ups, all in one spot. Most of the guys I talk to have pieces scattered across QuickBooks, a spreadsheet, their phone notes. This just pulls it together."**

---

### THE DIFFERENTIATOR

Pick one based on what you know:

**Using EagleView / RoofScope:**
> **"What are you spending on reports right now? Most guys are at $30–50 a pop and waiting on them. We're faster, same level of detail, and you get the CRM side on top of it."**

**No system at all:**
> **"Quick question — when a lead comes in, where does it live? Because most guys tell me it's half on a notepad, half in texts. That's usually where the money's leaking."**

**Doing solar too:**
> **"We've also got a solar design builder and solar reports built in, so if you run both sides, it's all in one place instead of bouncing between tools."**

---

### THE CLOSE

> **"I'm not asking you to commit to anything. 15-minute screen share, I show you the platform, you tell me if it's useful. If it's not, no worries. What's your week looking like?"**

**If they say "Just send me info":**
> **"Yeah I can do that. Honestly though, it's one of those things that clicks way faster when you see it than when you read about it. 15 minutes — is [day] or [day] easier?"**

**If they're not interested:**
> **"All good. It's roofmanager.ca if you ever want to poke around. Appreciate the time."**

*(Don't push. They might circle back.)*

---

## OBJECTIONS

### "What does it cost?"
> **"Depends on your plan and volume — I'll walk you through that on the demo. The math most guys land on: if it saves you a couple hours a week or you close one extra deal a month, it's already paying for itself. Let me just show you first."**

### "We already use [competitor]."
> **"Nice, what do you like about it? … Yeah that makes sense. The main difference is we do measurements and the full CRM in one place, so you're not stitching three tools together. Worth 15 minutes to compare?"**

### "I don't have time."
> **"Fair — that's kind of the whole reason I built it. 15 minutes this week, morning or afternoon?"**

### "I need to talk to my partner."
> **"Makes sense. Easier if you do the demo with me first though, then you've got something real to show them. Does [day] work for both of you?"**

### "Just email me."
> **"Sure, what's the best email? I'll send it over today. Let's pencil in a quick call a couple days out too — if you've already seen enough, we cancel it. Cool?"**

---

## VOICEMAIL (under 30 seconds)

> **"Hey [Name], Ethan with Roof Manager. We work with roofing companies around [area] — instant measurement reports, full CRM, all in one place. Wanted to put it on your radar. roofmanager.ca, or call me back at [number]. Talk soon."**

|--------|--------|
| Dials per day | 50–80 |
| Live conversations | 15–20 |
| Demos booked | 3–5 |
| Demos completed per week | 10–15 |
| Close rate on demos | 20–30% |

**Best windows:**
- 7:00–8:30 AM (before job sites)
- 12:00–1:00 PM (lunch)
- 4:30–6:00 PM (wrapping up)

Skip 9–11 AM and 2–4 PM — they're on roofs.

---

## TONE CHECK

- Peer, not salesperson.
- Steady pace. Not fast, not pushy.
- Their language: "guys," "jobs," "close deals."
- Okay with no. It keeps you credible.
- If you're not relaxed, they won't be either.
